Basic Information

My name is Vladimir Dabic, I'm 23 years old and I come from Belgrade, Serbia. I am a senior (fourth year) student in Computer science at "Racunarski fakultet" in Belgrade. I love programming, for me, it represents the way of life. The logic and thinking I got from it, helps me deal with everyday situations. I'm a very friendly and communicative person, and I love spending quality time with friends and family.

My interests

book

Books

When it comes to books, I am interested in anything that makes me say, "Neat" to myself, which could be anything on any given day. I love to learn, I love obscure details, and I love to research things that interest me. Most recently, those interests are: best practices about coding, design patterns in programming, agile techniques, project management, etc. Some of the best books on the subjects that I'm currently reading are: Clean Code: A Handbook of Agile Software Craftsmanship by Robert C. Martin and Managing the Unmanageable: Rules, Tools and Insights for Managing Software People and Teams by Mickey W. Mantle, Ron Lichty.

computer

Hardware

I simply love working with hardware, especially computers. Since a young age I've always been intrigued by computers, their power and their capabilities. Step by step I learned to assemble computers, customize them, test their limits and components, and use them for anything that comes to my mind. I'm also interested in building some simple things with Arduino boards, that will automate some stuff that I do on daily basics. My last project was a small fontain that I've built for my cat, nothing special, but she loved it!

accessibility

Martial arts

Every man has to find a way to get rid of negative energy and emotions, and this was mine. I say was because I'm not practicing martial arts for quite some time now. I had multiple injuries on both my shoulders and I had to make a long pause. I still watch it on TV every now and then and remember the days when I was in a top form and health. It helped me develop a different, better, mindset and improve my self-confidence. Some people might think that martial arts are a violent sport, but trust me, it isn't. There are far more injuries and dirty plays in some "less violent" sports like basketball and football. Martial arts are as its name says, an art.
